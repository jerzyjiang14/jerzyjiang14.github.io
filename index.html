Jerzy Jiang

zjiang71@alumni.jh.edu
Jerzy Jiang is currently the Deputy Director of the Capital Account Management Department at the State Administration of Foreign Exchange (SAFE). He previously served as a visiting student at Trinity College, Cambridge University, guided by Professor Sir James A. Mirrlees. In 2014, he worked as a Quant trading intern in London, focusing on Brent crude oil futures. From 2015 to 2016, he served as Manager of the Agricultural Products Department at the Zhengzhou Commodity Exchange, overseeing research on new futures products and market analysis of oilseed and sugar. Following this role, he joined SAFE. Between 2021 and 2024, Dr. Jiang served as an economist in the Capital Flow Unit of the Monetary and Capital Markets Department at the IMF Headquarters, where he was responsible for surveillance and assessment of exchange rate arrangements across over 30 countries. He is an Adjunct Research Fellow at the China Wealth Management 50 Forum and an Adjunct Professor at China Agricultural University. Dr. Jiang holds two doctoral degrees: one in Finance from the School of Economics and Management, Wuhan University, and another in International Affairs from the Paul H. Nitze School of Advanced International Studies, Johns Hopkins University. He also obtained MScs in Math & Finance, a B.A. in Finance, and a B.Eng. in Automation. Jerzy has received numerous research awards from the PBoC, SAFE, CSRC, JHU, and WHU. His research interests include capital flows, asset pricing, crypto transactions, derivatives regulation, etc.
